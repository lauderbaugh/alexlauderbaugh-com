A month into the deep half guard block. The first two weeks were terrible — every roll I gave up the back trying to enter, twice in one round to a blue belt who definitely shouldn't have been able to take it. The third week something shifted. The entries stopped being a choreography I was running and started being a thing my legs did while I worried about the next position.

The interesting part is that it didn't come from deeper understanding. It came from doing the wrong version of the entry maybe sixty times until it sanded itself into the right one. That's the bit I keep forgetting about jiu-jitsu: most of what looks like technique is just reps in the right shape.

The reason I'm writing this down is so that the next time I start a new positional block and the first two weeks feel terrible, I have a note from past-me saying: yes, that's what this is supposed to feel like. Keep going.
