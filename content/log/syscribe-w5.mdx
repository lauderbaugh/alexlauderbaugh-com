Spent the week ripping out the first pass at diff parsing. The naive approach — feeding the raw PR patch to the model — works for trivial changes but falls apart the moment a refactor touches more than three files. Reasoning gets diluted, edits sprawl.

Replaced it with a two-stage pipeline: a fast structural pass tags every hunk with a role (feature / refactor / move / config), then a slower pass writes only the entries that the role taxonomy says are user-visible. Output dropped from twenty-three noisy lines to four useful ones on the test repo. The right call almost always is to do less.
