Six months out from the Pan Pacific Masters Games. Two events on the track: the flying 200m sprint qualifier and the 500m time trial. I picked them because they sit at opposite ends of the same physiological line — one is twelve seconds of nothing-held-back, the other is thirty-five seconds of pacing under load.

I've raced road and crit since university but the track is new ground for me. Different bike, different fit, different mental model. The first session in March I qualified in the back half of the field. The most recent one I was top five. The arc is the point.

### Training block

Five days on the bike, two in the gym, one full rest. The gym work is unsexy — heavy hip thrust, single-leg press, banded glute work — but it's the difference between a sprint that opens at 1100w and one that opens at 1300w. I track power, sleep, and morning resting heart rate. Everything else is noise.

### Why it's a project

Because it has a deadline, an outcome that can be measured against the field, and a plan that can be wrong. That's a project. Calling it a hobby would lower the bar.
