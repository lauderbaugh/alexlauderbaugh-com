---
title: "Principles"
intro: "Things I believe about product, building, and showing up — held loosely enough to be wrong."
items:
  - heading: "The empty column is the most important column."
    body: "Every roadmap I've shipped that held up left a deliberate space for what the market would do. Filling it in advance is the most common mistake I see."
  - heading: "Strategy is a verb."
    body: "Slide decks are not strategy. Strategy is the set of decisions you've made about what not to do, and the experiments you've run to find out."
  - heading: "Customer interviews compound."
    body: "Thirty conversations in is when the pattern starts being signal. Three is noise. The teams that skip the first twenty-seven build the wrong thing."
  - heading: "Pruning beats adding."
    body: "Most product problems are excess. Most user complaints are about complexity, not missing features. The fastest improvement is usually a deletion."
  - heading: "Athletic discipline transfers."
    body: "The thing I've learned from training that I use every day at work: the plan is the point. Adjust it, but never abandon it on a hard morning."
  - heading: "Write it down or it didn't happen."
    body: "If a decision can't be recovered six months later from your own notes, the team will relitigate it. Cheap to write, expensive to skip."
---
